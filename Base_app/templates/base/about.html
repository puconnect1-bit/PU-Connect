How We Started

A Dorm Room Idea That Grew Into a Movement

It started when final-year students tried to sell their old textbooks before graduating. They posted on WhatsApp groups, got buried in noise, and eventually gave the books away for free out of frustration.

That weekend, the idea took shape: a dedicated, trusted space for campus commerce. No strangers, no spam — just students helping students.

Within three months of launching, over 800 students had signed up. Within a year, PU-Connect had processed more than GH₵ 500,000 in campus trades. Today, it's the go-to platform for everything from textbooks to tech, services to furniture — all within the university gates.

Our Purpose

Why PU-Connect Exists

Three core beliefs drive every decision we make — from the features we build to the policies we set.

Community First

Commerce is more than transactions — it's relationships. We build tools that strengthen the campus community, not just move goods between strangers.

Safety by Design

Every feature we ship is stress-tested against safety. Verified accounts and campus-only listings, aren't add-ons — they're foundations.

Student Empowerment

We believe every student has something of value to offer. PU-Connect is the stage — whether you're selling lecture notes or offering graphic design services.

What We Stand For

Our Values

These aren't poster words. They're the lens through which we make decisions every single day.

Transparency

No hidden fees, no secret policies. We tell you exactly how your data is used, what you're paying, and why we make the decisions we do.

Simplicity

University life is already complicated enough. Our platform is designed to be intuitive — post a listing in under 2 minutes, find what you need in seconds.

Inclusion

Whether you're a fresher or a PhD candidate, a Nursing student or an Engineering major — this marketplace belongs to all of you equally.

Continuous Improvement

We respond fast, listen to feedback, and iterate constantly. If something isn't working for our users, we fix it — not in months, but in days.
